📘 Case Study: From ANT to ScORCH

Simplifying Automation for Tax Infrastructure

🏢 Organization

A technology partner supporting the Portuguese Tax Office,
responsible for maintaining and deploying critical systems
in a regulated, high-stakes environment.

⚠️ The Challenge

- Legacy ANT scripts powering build and deployment pipelines had become:
  • Verbose and difficult to maintain
  • Opaque to testers and non-developer stakeholders
  • Fragile under change, with poor auditability

- Operational teams struggled to understand or safely modify automation logic.
- Onboarding new contributors required deep XML knowledge and tribal context.

🔄 The Migration

- ScORCH was introduced as a lightweight, declarative alternative.
- Initial training focused on:
  • Plugin structure (SP_-())
  • Core verbs (Task, Manual, Message, Sleep)
  • Parameterization via GetVar

- ANT scripts were incrementally replaced with ScORCH plugins,
  each representing a discrete job or deployment step.

✅ The Outcome

- Clarity: ScORCH’s syntax was immediately understandable—even to testers.
- Modularity: Plugins could be reused, extended, and versioned independently.
- Auditability: Each job step was logged and traceable, supporting compliance.
- Empowerment: Teams could author and evolve automation without deep XML expertise.

💬 Quote from the Field

“Its brilliance is in its simplicity.”
— Tester trained on ScORCH during the migration

🔧 Example Migration

Before (ANT fragment):

<target name="deploy">
  <exec executable="scp">
    <arg value="${build.artifact}" />
    <arg value="user@host:/opt/app/" />
  </exec>
</target>

After (ScORCH plugin):

SP_DEPLOY-ARTIFACT() {
  GetVar -pattern "Artifact" -name file_Artifact -default "/opt/app/build.jar"
  Task "scp ${file_Artifact} user@host:/opt/app/"
}

📈 Lessons Learned

- ScORCH is not just a tool—it’s a language for automation.
- Declarative plugins reduce onboarding time and increase operational confidence.
- Even in regulated environments, simplicity can be a strategic advantage.
