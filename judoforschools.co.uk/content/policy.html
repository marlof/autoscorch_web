Privacy Policy

How we use the data you supply

The categories of information that we collect, process and hold include:

  • Personal information, in the form of:
    • Parent/guardian details;
    • Student details.
  • Special category data, which consists of any pertinent medical data relating to the student.

Why we collect and use this information

We use the personal information data:
  • for contact in cases of emergency only;
  • to comply with the law regarding data sharing.

We use the special category data:
  • to provide appropriate pastoral care;
  • to comply with the law regarding data sharing.

The lawful basis on which we use this information

We collect and use student information on a lawful basis:
  • to ensure that we are fully aware of any conditions which may effect that student whilst in our care;
  • to protect the health and welfare of students.

Collecting student information

Whilst the majority of student information you provide to us is mandatory, some of it is provided to us on a voluntary basis. In order to comply with the General Data Protection Regulation, we will inform you whether you are required to provide certain student information to us or if you have a choice in this.

Storing data

All sensitive data is removed before being archived; we hold archived data for a fixed term to comply with the standards set out by HM Revenue and Customs.

Who we share information with

We never share any information, parent/guardian or student, with third parties, nor do we use any data for marketing purposes, direct or otherwise.

Requesting access to your personal data

Under data protection legislation, parents and students have the right to request access to information about them that we hold. To make a request for your personal information, contact the Data Protection Officer: info@judoforschools.co.uk

You also have the right to:
  • object to processing of personal data that is likely to cause, or is causing, damage or distress;
  • prevent processing for the purpose of direct marketing; object to decisions being taken by automated means;
  • in certain circumstances, have inaccurate personal data rectified, blocked, erased or destroyed; and
  • claim compensation for damages caused by a breach of the Data Protection regulations.
